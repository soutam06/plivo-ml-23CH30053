LLM 2000-Step Speedrun Summary

This report details the architectural choices, optimization strategies, and iterative experimentation used to minimize Bits Per Byte (BPB) on the provided GPT model within the strict 2M parameter and 2,000 step budget limits.

This submission was highly collaborative. The Human Developer (User) handled environment configuration, WSL interactions, version control management, and the execution of the training loops. The AI Coding Agent researched the "nanoGPT speedrun" state-of-the-art, hypothesized architectural upgrades, wrote the PyTorch implementations for RMSNorm and SwiGLU, and tuned the hyperparameter schedule. Together, this iterative feedback loop safely explored the parameter limit boundaries and successfully reduced the baseline BPB.

1. Final Model Architecture (The 1.29M Sweet Spot)

Our experimentation proved that maximizing the 2,000,000 parameter budget (either by scaling width or scaling depth) is detrimental under a strict 2,000 step limit. The larger models were sample-inefficient and ran out of time to converge. Our absolute best architecture is a highly-efficient 1.29M parameter model.

  • Parameter Scaling: We maintained 4 layers, 4 heads, and 160 embedding dimensions.
  • Weight Tying: We set tie_weights = True to share the token embeddings with the output linear head. This freed up massive parameter overhead, making the model leaner.
  • RMSNorm: We replaced standard LayerNorm with RMSNorm, which removes mean-centering and reduces computational overhead while preserving training stability.
  • SwiGLU Activation: We replaced the standard GELU Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) with a Gated Linear Unit (SwiGLU). SwiGLU creates sparser activations and yields superior performance per parameter.

2. Optimizer & Training Regimen

To maximize learning in exactly 2,000 steps, the training script was overhauled:

  • AdamW Optimizer: Replaced standard Adam with AdamW, utilizing a weight_decay of 0.01 for better regularization.
  • OneCycleLR Scheduler: Replaced the constant learning rate with a dynamic schedule. The LR warms up to an aggressive peak of 2e-3 for the first 10% of steps, then cosine-decays down. This enables the model to rapidly traverse the loss landscape early on and finely converge at the end.
  • Gradient Clipping: Implemented a max norm of 1.0 to prevent exploding gradients at the higher peak learning rate.

Why This Strategy is Optimal

In a heavily constrained environment (120 minutes wall-clock, CPU-only, 2000 steps), the primary bottleneck is how much information the model can absorb per step. By aggressively scaling parameters up to ~1.9M (in Runs 4 and 6), we discovered that larger models are too sample-inefficient to converge in 2,000 steps. The optimal metric strategy is to take a highly efficient smaller architecture (1.29M params with SwiGLU/RMSNorm) and push the learning rate aggressively high (2e-3 via OneCycleLR). This approach perfectly balances expressivity and convergence speed.
