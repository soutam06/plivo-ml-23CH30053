Our best configuration achieves its Bits Per Byte (BPB) by efficiently maximizing the 2,000-step and 2,000,000-parameter caps. We scaled the GPT architecture up to 1.83M parameters by using 192 embedding dimensions, 6 attention heads, and 4 layers. To squeeze maximum utility out of the parameter budget, we tied the token and output embeddings. We modernized the architecture by replacing standard LayerNorm with RMSNorm and the GELU MLP with SwiGLU, both of which provide better parameter efficiency and gradient flow. Crucially, we applied QK-Norm (RMSNorm on Queries and Keys) inside the self-attention blocks to stabilize attention at high learning rates. For training, we abandoned the baseline's constant learning rate and vanilla Adam. Instead, we used AdamW (with 0.01 weight decay) and a OneCycleLR scheduler, which warmed up the learning rate for the first 10% of steps to a peak of 1e-3 before cosine-decaying it. This aggressive scheduling, combined with gradient clipping (max norm 1.0), allowed the model to rapidly traverse the loss landscape and converge optimally within exactly 2,000 steps. The BPB plateaus because we are bounded by the 2M parameter limit and the small sequence length (128) of the naive byte-level tokenizer, which struggles to compress complex multi-byte Devanagari characters over long contexts.
