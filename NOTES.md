Our absolute best configuration achieves a Bits Per Byte (BPB) of 2.1867 by prioritizing sample-efficiency and advanced initialization over maximizing the 2,000,000-parameter cap. We discovered that pushing the parameter count to 1.8M+ (either by scaling width or depth) prevented the model from converging within the strict 2,000-step budget. Therefore, we heavily optimized a smaller 1.33M parameter architecture (4 layers, 4 heads, 160 embedding dimensions). To gain expressivity without bloating the model, we kept the token and output embeddings untied. We modernized the architecture by replacing standard LayerNorm with RMSNorm and utilizing a Squared ReLU (`F.relu(x)**2`) MLP, which is computationally faster and highly regularizing. Crucially, we zero-initialized all projection layers so the transformer blocks started as perfect identity functions, ensuring flawless gradient flow at step 0. For training, we used AdamW (0.01 weight decay) and a OneCycleLR scheduler with an aggressive peak learning rate of 2e-3. The zero-init stabilization, combined with the aggressive scheduling, allowed the 1.33M model to rapidly traverse the loss landscape and converge optimally within exactly 2,000 steps. The BPB plateaus because we are bounded by the 2000-step limit and the small sequence length (128) of the naive byte-level tokenizer, which inherently limits contextual compression of multi-byte characters.
