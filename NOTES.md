Our best configuration achieves its Bits Per Byte (BPB) of 2.1969 by prioritizing sample-efficiency over maximizing the 2,000,000-parameter cap. We discovered that pushing the parameter count to 1.8M+ (either by scaling width or depth) prevented the model from converging within the strict 2,000-step budget. Therefore, we optimized a smaller 1.29M parameter architecture (4 layers, 4 heads, 160 embedding dimensions). To squeeze maximum utility out of this budget, we tied the token and output embeddings. We modernized the architecture by replacing standard LayerNorm with RMSNorm and the GELU MLP with SwiGLU, both of which provide better parameter efficiency and gradient flow. For training, we abandoned the baseline's constant learning rate and vanilla Adam. Instead, we used AdamW (with 0.01 weight decay) and a OneCycleLR scheduler, which warmed up the learning rate to an aggressive peak of 2e-3 before cosine-decaying it. This aggressive scheduling, combined with gradient clipping (max norm 1.0), allowed the highly-efficient 1.29M model to rapidly traverse the loss landscape and converge optimally within exactly 2,000 steps. The BPB plateaus because we are bounded by the 2000-step limit and the small sequence length (128) of the naive byte-level tokenizer, which struggles to compress complex multi-byte characters over long contexts.
